What is Computer Information Systems?

Computer Information Systems (CIS) is a field that deals with the use of computer systems to collect, organize, and distribute data for various purposes. CIS professionals design, develop, and maintain software systems, databases, networks, and web applications that support the information needs of organizations.

Benefits of the Program

Learn how to apply computer systems effectively in a business context. Gain a dual skill set that blends technical expertise and business savvy in software creation, system evaluation and project coordination.

  • Work Experience: You get the opportunity to work in the field before graduating, plus looks great on your resume.
  • Business and IT: You can get the best of both worlds with a blend of business and computing courses, which will equip you with a double skill set.
  • Networking: Lots of opportunities to meet people in the same field to help you out.

Computer Information Systems vs. Computer Science

Computer Information System

  • Focuses more on the practical applications of technology in the business world.

Computer Science

  • Focuses more on the theory behind computer science disciplines.

Requirements for CIS

High School Applicant

  • English 30 -1
  • Social Studies 30 -1
  • Takes your two highest 30 level classes grades and get the average for it.

Mature Applicant

  • Takes the average of the two highest grades of University level classes.

Post Secondary Applicant

  • Competitive GPA calculated from four most recent transferable post-secondary courses (minimum 12 credits) completed by December 31st.
